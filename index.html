Matching Pennies

Let's play a game. You and your opponent (the computer) have coins, and the two of you will flip your coins at the same time. You win a point if both coins land on the same side (both coins could land Heads, for example). Your opponent wins otherwise.

Both you and your opponent are allowed to pick the bias of your coin. For the first game, your opponent picks a bias first. You can see the bias of your opponent's coin before you make your move, so pick your bias wisely.

After you pick your bias, our simulator will run a 100 coin tosses, and will output your score at the end.
